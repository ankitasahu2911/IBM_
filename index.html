ABOUT US

ZeroHunger is the United Nations' second Sustainable Development Goal, aiming to eradicate hunger, ensure food security, improve nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture by 2030. It focuses on providing everyone, especially the poor and vulnerable, with sufficient, safe, and nutritious food all year round, while also supporting sustainable farming practices.

OUR MISSION AND VISION

To develop an innovative web platform that combats hunger by connecting communities, providing resources, and promoting sustainable agriculture. We envision a world where everyone has access to nutritious food, empowered by technology and community collaboration, to achieve Zero Hunger, ensuring food security and improved nutrition for all.

DONORS & VOLUNTEERS

Anyone Can Be a Food Donor.

Our donors play a crucial role in the battle against hunger. By contributing surplus food, they ensure that it reaches those who need it most, helping to reduce waste and feed the hungry. HELP/FAQs

What is Zero hunger?

Zero Hunger is a food recovery and food waste management network that helps connect individuals and businesses with excess food to people in need. We approach the issue of hunger with a sharing-economy model: the donation of excess food enables a vital, yet often wasted, resource to be used to its full capacity.

How ZH is different from others?

Our use of technology makes Zero Hunger unique as we are able to provide our services on-demand and trustable.

How will my donation be used?

Your donation will be used to support our mission and the various programs and initiatives that we have in place. Your donation will help us to continue providing assistance and support to those in need. You can find more information about our programs and initiatives on our website. If you have any specific questions or concerns, please feel free to contact us

What should I do if my food donation is near or past its expiration date?

We appreciate your willingness to donate, but to ensure the safety of our clients we can't accept food that is near or past its expiration date. We recommend checking expiration dates before making a donation or contact us for further guidance
